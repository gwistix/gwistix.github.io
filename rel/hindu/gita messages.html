Dhritarashtra
On the field of Dharma, in the field of Kuru, arrayed and ready for battle…
my people and also the Pandavs, what indeed did they do, Sanjaya?
Sanjaya
Seeing the Pandav soldiers in their ranks, Duryodhana then
approached his advisor, and the king spoke these words
Duryodhana
Behold this mighty army of the sons of Pandu, teacher
arrayed for battle by the son of Drupada, your capable disciple

Here are powerful warriors, equal to Bheema and Arjuna in battle:
…

Our strength is unlimited, protected by Bheeshma
But their strenth is limited, protected by Bheema
Arjuna
Place my chariot between the two armies, Achyuta

As I behold these men arrayed for battle
With whom should I fight in this war effort

Desiring to see those who have come together to fight
The wicked son of Dhritarashtra, they wish to please
Sanjaya
Thus spoke Hrisheekesha (Krishna) to Gudakeshena (Arjuna), son of Bharata,
having placed the best of the chariots between both armies

Bheeshma and Drona before all the great rulers
Then he said
